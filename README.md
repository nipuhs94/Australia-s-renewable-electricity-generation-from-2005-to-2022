# Australia-s-renewable-electricity-generation-from-2005-to-2022
This reflects a data-driven dashboard developed in Power BI for a business intelligence consultancy project and delivers strategic insights. 

Australia Renewable Energy Analysis and Forecasting Dashboard

Project Overview

This project was developed as part of a Business Intelligence consulting engagement to evaluate Australia’s transition towards renewable energy and assess future progress toward national renewable energy targets.

Using Power BI, the project combines government and international energy datasets to analyse historical renewable electricity generation trends, compare Australia’s performance with neighbouring countries, and forecast future renewable energy adoption through to 2030.

The project demonstrates the complete Business Intelligence lifecycle, including data acquisition, extraction, transformation, analysis, dashboard development, forecasting, and executive-level reporting.

Business Problem

As governments worldwide increase investment in sustainable energy initiatives, decision-makers require reliable insights into renewable energy adoption and future energy trends.

The objective of this project was to answer four key business questions:

1. What proportion of Australia’s electricity generation comes from renewable energy sources, and how has this changed since 2005?
2. How has the composition of renewable energy generation evolved over time?
3. How does Australia compare with neighbouring countries in renewable energy generation?
4. What is the projected level of renewable energy generation in Australia by 2030?

The findings were designed to support evidence-based decision-making for policymakers, energy planners, and stakeholders involved in renewable energy strategy.

Key Findings

Renewable Energy Growth

Australia’s renewable electricity generation increased significantly between 2005 and 2022, rising from approximately 9% to 36% of total electricity generation.

Changing Energy Mix

Solar and wind energy became the primary drivers of renewable energy growth, while hydroelectric generation remained relatively stable throughout the study period.

International Comparison

Australia demonstrated strong growth but remained behind regional leaders such as New Zealand, which consistently generated more than 80% of electricity from renewable sources.

Future Forecast

Forecast modelling indicates continued growth in renewable electricity generation, with projected renewable shares reaching approximately 55–65% by 2030 under current trends.
